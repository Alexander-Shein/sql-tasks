Create a SQL query to find the employee that sold products for the largest amount:

| EmployeeId | Employee Full Name | Amount, $ |
___________________________

SELECT TOP(1) [emp].[EmployeeID], [emp].[FirstName], 
SUM([odsDet].[UnitPrice] * [odsDet].[Quantity] - ([odsDet].[UnitPrice] * [odsDet].[Quantity]) * [odsDet].[Discount]) AS [Amount, $] 
FROM [Northwind].[dbo].[Employees] [emp]
JOIN [Northwind].[dbo].[Orders] [ods]
ON [emp].[EmployeeID] = [ods].[EmployeeID]
JOIN [Northwind].[dbo].[Order Details] [odsDet]
ON [odsDet].[OrderID] = [ods].[OrderID]
GROUP BY [emp].[EmployeeID], [emp].[FirstName]
HAVING SUM([odsDet].[UnitPrice] * [odsDet].[Quantity] - ([odsDet].[UnitPrice] * [odsDet].[Quantity]) * [odsDet].[Discount]) > 10000
ORDER BY [Amount, $] DESC
